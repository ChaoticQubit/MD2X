## Archetype: explainer

For teaching how something works — concept, system, or API made intuitive and explorable.

**Shell:** sidebar. **Render mode:** hybrid (default).

**Structure.** Open with a TL;DR `summary` (what it is, why it matters, in 2-3 lines). Then build understanding in layers: a `collapsible` request-path / lifecycle walkthrough the reader can expand step by step, `tabs` for config or language variants, and a live interactive demo where they can poke the real behavior. Weave in a `glossary` with hover definitions for jargon, an `svg-figure` for the mental model, and close with an FAQ as `collapsible` Q&A. Sidebar nav follows the concept's layers.

**Favor.** `live-demo` as the centerpiece, `flowchart` for the request/data path, `svg-figure` for the model, and `comparison` to contrast approaches. Hybrid is the point — let readers learn by doing.

**Avoid.** Don't hand-wave or invent behavior the source doesn't describe; if the demo simplifies reality, say so. Keep terminology consistent with the source.
